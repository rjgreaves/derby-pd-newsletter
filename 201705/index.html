Let's Go Ride a Bike

Our Cycle Day at Markeaton Park on Wednesday, 12 April was a huge success. Everyone had fun and enjoyed new experiences. 'Let's go ride a bike' provides one-to-one cycling training for riders with a disability. Riders can access up to three 1 to 1 training sessions that are tailored to the individual's needs and delivered at a time to suit them by qualified trainers. To find out more contact Christine Kelly at Markeaton Park - christine.kelly@derby.gov.uk (07974148875)
If this appeals to you, remember the Ashbourne Branch have organised some cycling days at Markeaton Park in the summer and have invited any of our members to join them They like to cycle to the coffee shop, have a coffee and then cycle back again or you can do your own thing and take off round the park on a bike by yourself! Dates are 10 May, 7 June, 5 July 10.30am -12pm Any queries 01332 832777

The future of Attendance Allowance – a lifeline for people living with Parkinson’s – has been saved thanks to Parkinson’s UK campaigners in England. Result! Phil Reynolds, our Policy and Campaigns Adviser, explains “We sent our response to the consultation before Christmas. We met the minister in January to list our concerns. On 19 January, we found out Attendance Allowance would continue to be managed by central government.”
