How up to date are you?

As part of Parkinson’s UK commitment to the Information Standard scheme, our health information for the general public is reviewed at least once every three years. Our most up-to-date information is on the improved information and support section of our Parkinson’s UK website: parkinsons.org.uk/information-and-support.

Which topics have been updated?

  • Monitoring your Parkinson’s (formerly called Keeping a diary: people with Parkinson’s)
  • Keeping a diary: for carers
  • Daily living equipment
  • Mild memory and thinking problems

Disability Rights UK have helped with the annual update of our benefits, grants and loans information.

A wide range of topics are covered on information sheets and booklets from drug treatments ,surgical treatment, to looking after your bowel and bladder etc …..too many to list here.

Karen Bown will have printed versions of all information available on request and we often have them at the Branch meetings.

You are invited to attend a Carers Listening Event

Wednesday 29th November 2017 - Makeney Hall, Makeney Rd, Milford, Belper, DE56 0RS

The event is an opportunity to talk to you as a carer and get your feedback on:

  • Emergency Planning – what happens in the event you can't provide care?
  • Discharge Planning from hospital
  • Carers Pathway – what can carers expect?
  • Assistive Technologies – how technology can help you in your caring role
  • Joined Up Care Derbyshire – your view on the Sustainability and Transformation Plan for Derbyshire
  • We also want to 'thank you' for the work you do and have arranged for a light lunch to be available. Numbers will be limited to 40

The event starts at 10am and ﬁnish at 2pm and we hope that we have given enough notice for you to make suitable arrangements for the person you care for to be looked after whilst you are out. Travel expenses will be available at per mile if you are driving 45p and bus/train fares as required. In exceptional circumstances taxis will be refunded up to a maximum of £10 each way but this must be agreed in advance.

Southern Derbyshire Clinical Commissioning Group has allocated some charitable funds to hold two Listening Events for unpaid carers who care for an adult living in Derbyshire-the one in Belper is our nearest, but there is another in Chesterfield.

If you would like to attend, please contact. Tina Brown, Commissioning Manager, Southern Derbyshire CCG, First Floor, Cardinal Square, 10 Nottingham Road, Derby, DE1 3QT

"Taylor-made"

If any members belong to other groups, who may want to book a speaker for their meetings from time to time, Alan Taylor is willing to talk about his time working in the Derby Mayor's office. He spoke about this to one of our monthly meetings some time ago, and has since spoken at the Derby branch of the International Friendship Group. He is booked to speak again in November for another group, who will also give him a donation for the Parkinson’s Derby and District Branch.

Membership is open to all
What do members get?
  • 4 editions a year of our magazine - Parkinson. Each issue is full of experiences of people living with Parkinson's and current information about the condition.
  • 2 editions a year of our research magazine - Progress Providing updates on the studies we fund and details of how to get more involved with Parkinson's research.
  • Support on your doorstep- Local Branches where you can meet other people living with Parkinson's for support and enjoy many activities
  • Your voice heard. Help shape our future and campaign for a better quality of life for people living with Parkinson's.
