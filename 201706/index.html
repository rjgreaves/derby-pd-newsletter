A Terrific Railway Trip

A group from Derby Branch had a splendid day out at the Severn Valley Railway. After a nail-biting journey-we got lost-we finally arrived at the station and were asked to board as quickly as possible-not easy with Parkinson’s, but soon we were all in and were off! On arrival in Bridgnorth we had two hours to view, eat and wander, Perhaps this was not the best town for people with walking problems: there were steep hills to climb (helped by the Funicular Railway) and distances to travel ….but we did it. and showed everybody what can be done even with Parkinson’s. Our thanks to Peter Shaffrey for organising what proved to be a grand day out.

Put Parkinson's on the agenda this election

Our key issues

Protecting research in the Brexit deal
We want to make sure the UK keeps its vital life science partnerships with the EU to help find better treatments and a cure for Parkinson's.
Safeguarding disability benefits
Disability benefits are a lifeline for people with Parkinson's. But tougher eligibility rules and smaller benefits payments mean that many people struggle to cover the costs associated with the condition or being unable to work
There is an open letter asking party leaders to protect disability benefits. on the Parkinson’s UK website available for us to sign.
Improving mental health services for people with Parkinson's
Mental health problems are common in people with Parkinson's, yet they often go undiagnosed and untreated, leaving people without the support they need
The Blackpool Holiday Project
A volunteer support group of Parkinson’s UK organise an annual holiday in Blackpool for people affected by Parkinson’s. They offer a week of rest and therapies for people with Parkinson’s, their friends and carers each October at: The Savoy Hotel Queens Promenade Blackpool, FY2 9SJ The hotel is part of the Britannia group and the staff has undergone Parkinson’s awareness training.

Parkinson's Local Adviser
Karen Bown is one of Parkinson's local advisers, offering one-to-one information and support to people with Parkinson's, their families and carers across the UK. She can offer information and support to people at every stage of Parkinson's, whether they're newly diagnosed or starting to deal with more complex symptoms. One important part of the job is letting people know about services and support in the area from other agencies. They also let people know about other support from Parkinson's UK, including local groups, our helpline 0808 800 0303, website and publications.
